Introduction back to ToC

The TABULAE ontology is an extension of the PeGazUs vocabulary dedicated to the representation of information about land plots contained in the French 19th-century land registry documents. It enables the description of land plots, their nature (with over 150 controlled values) and addresses, of the associated taxpayers, and the changes and events affecting these plots over time. Their evolution is described at the attribute level. The ontology also models the sources involved (mutation registers, section registers, cadastral index maps) and provides support to described the processing of the documents, by hand or automatically, including images classification, information extraction and named entity recognition in digitised documents. The TABULAE ontology re-uses concepts and properties of the PeGazUs, Time, RiC-O, and PROV-O ontologies.
